Telecommunications Technology Installation

Microwave Technology

A microwave link is a communications system that uses radio waves to transmit video, audio, or data over long distances. Modern microwave links can carry up to 400Mbps with IP header compression techniques. Line-of-sight is crucial for communication.

  • Ultra High Frequency (UHF) 0.3-3 GHz
  • Super High Frequency (SHF) 3-30 GHz
  • Extremely High Frequency (EHF) 30-300 GHz

Signal Cellular Boosters

Devices carry and amplify weak signals in rural or remote areas, sending them to the nearest cell tower. Common uses include corporate networks, broadcast television, and last-mile communication for ISPs and cellular operators.

  • Rural Farm Signal Boosting
  • Office/Home Signal Boosting
  • Geographical Area Coverage

Optic Fibre Cabling

Fiber-optic cables transmit data over long distances using light signals. They offer high bandwidth, immunity to electromagnetic interference, longer transmission distance, and higher security compared to copper cables.

  • High bandwidth capacity
  • Immune to electromagnetic interference
  • Long transmission distances
  • More secure than copper cables
